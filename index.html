Giancarlo-Fajardo - creative graphic design portfolio for print and web

As viewed on: 1/18/2012
Brand
Loud, aggressive, attention grabbing. Many bright colors on an earth-tone background which causes the figures in the foreground to be even more prominent, yet is not just a boring background by use of texturing. Hire him and your product will get people's attention.
Functionality
Navigation at top is creative and different, yet usable format. Each navigation button takes the user to specific parts of the website that are relevant to the brand being presented by having the content within those pages clearly laid out. Making a selection in the work gallery page causes that item to come to the foreground and everything else dims out until the selection is closed. This does well in giving the user the ability to focus his or her entire attention to that one selection. Some ability to view the selection in such enhanced detail among other ones may be lost with that function, however, one can still see all the selections as a group within the 'work gallery' page. Overall, the website serves both the designer's and potential clients' goals while giving an edgy and aggressive presentation.
Interactivity
The contact page includes a short 3-part form in which to fill out one's information for a price quote for the designer's services. Mouse-over and link-selected effects throughout
Navigation
A small colored tab appears on the button of the omnipresent navigation section for the selected page as an indicator of location on the site, yet it isn't enough to stand out from the attention-grabbing colors and figure of the featured graphic to really notice without looking for such a cue. This being said, the site is simple enough for the user that he or she can rely on the aforementioned cues in order to navigate the site effectively.

Accent Creative | Art Direction & Design

As viewed on: 1/18/2012
Brand
To the point, no flashy stuff. Clean. Pleasant but gives a feeling of professionalism. Chooses black on light, earth-tone colors as to not be an eyesore except for links that have a red/orange color. The 'about' section includes identical descriptions side by side with one in English and the other in Spanish. Seems to be available to clientele in either category.
Functionality
Plenty of links on the sidebar for secondary pages which contain much related examples of work. The website shows things off effectively, however, regarding the about/contact page's bilingual nature, it would be safe to assume that he speaks and works in either language, and that he is seeking to reach out to clients in both categories; however, any ability to select full website navigation in Spanish, which would be step one in accommodating that entire set of users, seems to be absent.
Interactivity
There is not a whole lot of interactivity going on here aside from a mouse-over effect or two and a color change when a link is selected. It seems to be what you see is what you get.
Navigation
The red/orange contrast color makes it very obvious, yet not distractingly so, as to what is a link before even having to mouse-over. Hover creates an orange box around the link. Very apparent which link the cursor is over, but once selected the box turns to black, seems to be in keeping with the clean look but still is a good reference for where the user is in the site.

Dale Harris - Creative branding & graphic design

As viewed on: 1/19/2012
Brand
Fun, Bold, Artsy. The background color is a dark, fairly neutral color mostly plain with some graphics, and the text and some elements within the graphics having bright, almost neon colors causes them to stand out. Capability with being simple yet effective and eye catching.
Functionality
A Main block with three smaller blocks under it. The portfolio itself was contained to another block below those. One can progress through the work examples in a sort of slide-show fashion, but the controls for such were not visible, yet there ended up being controls, however their existence was not apparent until the user moves his or her mouse over to the left or right side and arrows appear. The presentation is simple and effective, however, it would be better if the controls for the progression through the works were more apparent. The contact section of the page has the usual information, but only a link to send him an email is present rather than the address itself being displayed. Clicking on the link causes whatever off-line email-client software to open. This is both frustrating and ineffective for the user who does not use such software, and the lack of a separate written-out email address causes a problem for those trying to contact the designer by such means.
Interactivity
The user has the ability to control the progress through the works gallery; however, there isn't a way to jump from one thing to another if they are not adjacent.
Navigation
The site lacks a clickable logo or 'home' button to direct the user to the home page, however, relevant links do not take the user away from the main page as all of the aspects of the page are contained on it, so one really isn't that necessary. In fact, links higher up on the page for the work gallery below just cause the page to automatically move down and the user's attention is then drawn to that object.

Mike | Creative Mints: Freelance Web Design

As viwed on: 1/19/2012
Brand
Fun. Clean and creative but not too flashy. The colors and featured graphic chosen for the fold make it stand out but not in an overbearing way that would cause focus to be taken away from the content. Attention can still be given to the pertinent content on the page. Fun but effective.
Functionality
The page is divided into three main sections differing in background color and featured graphic as well as content. The different sections can be accessed by means of the navigation buttons at the top or by scrolling down the page. The layout is simple and consistent this way, yet the coloration and graphic differences between the sections help to differentiate the content.
Interactivity
The fold contains an accordion-type viewer with different tabs that make animated transitions when selected.
Navigation
Clicking on a work example causes the selected example to open up in the center by means of some sort of viewer. Controls for the progression and direction of the presentation are included, as well as a fraction like '1/30' to indicate how many examples there are and on which among them the user is currently viewing. One is not bound to this method of browsing, since he or she can easily exit out, by using the close button or just by simply clicking anywhere, and select different one from the group to perhaps make a selection based on a more holistic comparison among the examples. The home button exists at the top, but remains up there while the user scrolls down through the rest of the site, however, the functionality remains by a "top" button stylized as an arrow pointing up which takes the user back to what is essentially the home page.

Marius Roosendaal's portfolio

As viewed on: 1/19/2012
Brand
Artistic, photographic, sleek. The website is mainly white on black and many of the work examples are photographs of the website open on different machines. Perhaps this gives more of a focus to the existence of an experience being created than just a product by picturing the product displayed on devices people would use to view it.
Functionality
Clicking on one of the work example elements takes the user to another page where there is a page by page scroll-down example of the website design as well as additional images which show the site in different artistic sort of photographs. The very same array of selections can be found at the bottom of one of these pages as is found on the fold of the homepage.
Interactivity
Moving one's mouse over an image causes the cursor to change to a magnifying glass, which once clicked on, the image is enlarged in a separate viewer with the rest of the website dimmed as to bring the image to the center of focus.
Navigation
The same example selections as are on the fold of the homepage being available to the user at the bottom of each of the separate example pages gives the user the ability to navigate from there, however, it seems that since one has to scroll down all the way until this function is revealed, most users would rather and more frequently use the in-browser back button instead; however, this is of course speculation.

Paul Noble - Interactive Designer

As viewed on: 1/19/2012
Brand
Professional, effective, theater-esque. The way that the lighting fades from the center element to the sides and then dim around the border of the background gives it a sort of theater-lighting or gallery feel to the site.
Functionality
The unique navigation elements within this site are very functional and useful, yet they also give the site some of its creative flair which serves as a function of the site it itself. The user navigates through the different areas being clearly shown what to focus on by the lighting effects present on the page.
Interactivity
Most of the very interesting interactivity is contained within the navigation elements of the site. Moving one's mouse over the bars of the navigation map gives a pop-up preview of the page for which that bar represents; however, one cannot click directly on that and expect to be transported to that area.
Navigation
On the side are buttons similar in image and function to the arrow keys on a keyboard that the user can press either those or the actual keyboard arrows to navigate around the website. Next to that there is also a set of bars which have an almost map-like feel which the user can use as an indication as to his or her location within the site. A section highlighted in white, with the other parts remaining gray, sort of serves as the compass to this aforementioned map, enabling the user to know where they are in relation to adjacent page elements. There are also arrows on each of the 4 sides of the main center viewing area with which the user can use to get around as well.
